Hyperfov

Tools for exploring the internet with greater speed and dexterity.

Browsing the internet is a fight for our attention. Hyperfov is a new way to slice through the noise and move at the speed of curiosity.

↓

A website's external links provide context and opportunity to explore, yet they're never seen as one collection.

Links are buried in content and obscured by vague hypertext. But they're the key to moving quickly between websites and finding new destinations. We need a new way to see how a website is situated without needing interact with popups, distracting signup buttons, and cookie banners.

Hyperfov provides a bird's eye view of a website and everywhere it links to, so you can immediately find what you're looking for and new directions to explore.

Despite their rich, interlinked structure, websites can only be explored linearly.

Navigating around a website is a slow process. It takes time to find what you're looking for, move to the next page, scan it, and repeat the process. We need a new way to browse that allows for exploration down multiple paths, preserving context over time.

Hyperfov creates "trails" as you browse, so you can see exactly how you got to the page you're on.

Search engines are generic, searching everything rather than a specific cluster of the internet.

Even though the Big G knows all our preferences, it still feeds us trashy-clickbait-popup-filled websites as primary search results. It attempts to build a global measure of quality, not an individual one. As we browse we develop a cluster of content we've seen, think is higher quality, and can use to search from again.

Hyperfov is a search engine for content from your own known and adjacent websites, so you can search just a small section of the internet using your own metric of quality.
